Internship Fall 2022

Position: Software Dev Engineer Intern
Payments Department
Team : Payments Beach Processors

During my internship at Amazon, I gained invaluable industry experience and insights by actively participating in real-world projects. One significant aspect of my experience was gaining a comprehensive understanding of my team's placement within the Payments department and delving into the diverse roles and responsibilities held by both other teams and my teammates. I had the opportunity to work on two projects during my time at Amazon.
Project 1:
The Problem: Many other teams have components that communicate with our services. Every time one of these teams wants to see logs, they would have to create a ticket to our team asking for logs. As more teams onboard our services, manually addressing each of these tickets becomes more of a burden on the team. Even more so if they need the logs after each change to their service while debugging/development.
Solution: Create a central point for beta logs so that teams can ask for one-time access to the service. This way, other teams only have to submit a ticket one time and will be able to see logs indefinitely.
This project allowed me to explore various AWS Cloud Services as I had to use them to create the service. I was given the opportunity to communicate with external partners and other teams.
Project 2:
The Problem: Certain Payment Processors were migrating to a new infrastructure for internal reasoning. One of the legacy payment processors was split into two processors for the same purpose.
Solution: Find out why the processor was split into two and migrate the functionality to the new infrastructure
During this project, I was able to figure out a way to merge the 2 processors into 1. I was able to conduct end-to-end testing and was able to work with several teammates to do this migration.
